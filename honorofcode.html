School


Code.org Education Outreach at School 101.


We chose secondary school No. 101 located in Yeni Surakhani as our school.
On 31 october, our team visited School 101 to introduce Code.org education. The session included hands-on coding exercises, sparking enthusiasm and curiosity among the children. Their keen interest highlighted the impact of early exposure to technology. This initiative at School 101 is a testament to the positive change achievable through tech education outreach programs, fostering a generation with essential skills for the digital age.
Everything was better than we expected. As we concluded the presentation, expressing our gratitude to the staff and teachers for their collaboration, it was evident that this session had not only introduced students to the possibilities within technology but also laid the foundation for their future exploration and success.


Presentation


We all had role. Yusif and Ruslan were the presentors, and Seyid was video and photographer, Ismayil was slide control and kept students in interest for Code.org


But we all had just one main role which was to teach students Code.org.
Our presentation at School 101 commenced with a spotlight on ADA University, setting the stage for an exploration into the world of coding and technology. On October 31, our team visited School 101 in Yeni Surakhani, choosing it as the ideal location for introducing Code.org education. Beginning with ADA University, we highlighted the significance of higher education in technology and its role in shaping future careers. The transition seamlessly led us to Code.org, a global movement celebrated in over 180 countries. The interactive session involved hands-on coding exercises that captivated the students' interest and curiosity. The positive energy in the room was palpable as students actively participated in solving coding challenges, showcasing an aptitude for technology. The initiative at School 101 became a living testament to the transformative impact achievable through tech education outreach programs.


Code.org


Code.org-Minecraft(Steve) tasks


After introducing ADA university, we started talking about Code.org. The students were very interested in it and we introduced them to coding as much as possible. We looked at several problems with the students and analyzed how they would solve them by giving them difficult codes. The students were very successful in deciphering the codes and finding the way out. Through Code.org, several students have found their interests and determined their future careers. We were proud to have both us and Code.org involved in the children's decisions.

The collaborative effort between ADA University and Code.org was instrumental in inspiring and guiding these students. As educators, it was a source of pride to witness the positive influence on their decisions and the profound impact on their trajectories.


Certification


Rewarding students with the certificates given to us by Code org at the end


The pinnacle of our engagement with Code.org was the rewarding certification bestowed upon students. At the conclusion of the program, students were presented with certificates, a tangible recognition of their dedication and accomplishments in the realm of coding and technology. This gesture not only celebrated their achievements but also served as a symbolic testament to their newfound skills and the impact of the collaborative efforts between ADA University and Code.org. The certificates, provided by Code.org, stand as a testament to the students' commitment and the success of our joint endeavors in shaping a digitally literate and empowered generation.
